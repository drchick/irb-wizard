NOT RESEARCH

Activity Does Not Meet the Definition of Research

No IRB review required. This is the best possible outcome for activities that are not systematic investigations intended to produce generalizable knowledge.

Example Scenario
Scenario 1 ‚Äî Curriculum Evaluation

Dr. Reyes evaluates a new active-learning format in her Statistics course

She surveys students at the end of the semester about their experience with flipped classroom instruction. Results will be used internally to decide whether to adopt the format department-wide. She does not plan to publish or present the findings beyond a departmental report.

Scenario 2 ‚Äî Program Quality Improvement

UB IT department surveys employees about satisfaction with new software rollout

The survey is operational in nature ‚Äî used solely to improve the IT helpdesk process. No intent to generalize findings beyond UB's internal operations.

Why It Gets This Classification
  • The activity is not designed to develop or contribute to generalizable knowledge
  • Findings will not be published, presented at conferences, or disseminated beyond the institution
  • The primary purpose is operational, instructional, or administrative improvement
  • The activity may be journalism, oral history, biography, or public benefit programs

NOT HUMAN SUBJECTS

Research, but No Human Subjects Involvement

The activity is research, but does not involve human subjects as defined by 45 CFR 46. No full IRB review required ‚Äî but a determination letter is recommended.

Example Scenarios
Scenario 1 ‚Äî Secondary Data (De-identified)

Analysis of publicly available, de-identified patient records from CMS

Dr. Okonkwo downloads a CMS claims dataset. All records are fully de-identified per HIPAA Safe Harbor. He analyzes patterns in hospital readmission rates. No interaction with individuals; no identifiable private information.

Scenario 2 ‚Äî Cadaveric / Biological Specimens (No Identifiers)

Pathological analysis of archived, anonymized tissue samples

A researcher studies tumor microenvironments using 15-year-old biobank samples that were collected under a prior IRB protocol. The samples have no remaining link to any individual.

Scenario 3 ‚Äî Observational Data of Public Behavior

Analysis of public social media posts about climate change attitudes

Researcher scrapes publicly visible Twitter/X posts using a keyword search. No interaction with users; data is publicly accessible and not private in any reasonable expectation.

Why It Gets This Classification
  • No living individuals are subject to intervention or interaction by the researcher
  • No identifiable private information is obtained about living individuals
  • Secondary data is fully de-identified and cannot be re-linked
  • OR: uses only publicly available information that subjects have no reasonable expectation of privacy for

EXEMPT

Exempt from Full IRB Review ‚Äî Limited Review Required

The research involves human subjects but falls into one of the federal exempt categories. An IRB administrator must confirm the exemption ‚Äî researchers cannot self-certify.

Common Exempt Scenarios
Category 2 ‚Äî Survey / Interview Research

Anonymous online survey of UB undergraduate stress and coping strategies

Prof. Diaz administers a 25-question anonymous Qualtrics survey to 200 undergraduates about academic stress. No identifying information collected. Topics are not sensitive enough to warrant serious harm if disclosed. Results will be published in a peer-reviewed journal.

Category 1 ‚Äî Normal Educational Practices

Comparing two instructional approaches in a UB education course

A faculty researcher compares student outcomes across two sections of the same course ‚Äî one using traditional lecture, one using peer instruction. All activities are standard educational practice. No sensitive data collected.

Category 4 ‚Äî Secondary Research with Identifiable Data

Analysis of existing identified medical records for complication rates

Researcher accesses existing patient records under a HIPAA waiver to study post-surgical complication rates. No new data collection; secondary use of records originally collected for clinical care.

The 8 Exempt Categories (2018 Common Rule)
  • ¬ß46.104(d)(1) ‚Äî Normal educational practices in established curricula
  • ¬ß46.104(d)(2) ‚Äî Surveys, interviews, observations of public behavior (adults, no more than minimal risk)
  • ¬ß46.104(d)(3) ‚Äî Benign behavioral interventions with adults (brief, harmless, consent optional)
  • ¬ß46.104(d)(4) ‚Äî Secondary research with identifiable data/specimens (HIPAA authorization or waiver)
  • ¬ß46.104(d)(5) ‚Äî Federal demonstration projects studying public benefit/service programs
  • ¬ß46.104(d)(6) ‚Äî Taste and food quality evaluation, consumer acceptance studies
  • ¬ß46.104(d)(7) ‚Äî Storage or maintenance for secondary research (with broad consent)
  • ¬ß46.104(d)(8) ‚Äî Secondary research with broad consent
‚ÑπÔ∏è Important: Categories 7 and 8 (broad consent) are new under the 2018 revision. Categories 1‚Äì3 do not apply to research involving prisoners. Categories 2 and 3 may still require limited IRB review for privacy safeguards.

EXPEDITED

Expedited Review ‚Äî Minimal Risk, Specific Categories

Research that presents no more than minimal risk and falls into one of nine expedited review categories. Reviewed by one or more designated IRB members rather than the full board.

Example Scenarios
Category 6 ‚Äî Voice/Video Recordings (not exempt)

Semi-structured interviews with UB faculty about pandemic-era remote teaching

Dr. Chen conducts 60-minute recorded Zoom interviews with 20 faculty members. Topics include professional challenges, institutional support, and burnout. Recordings will be transcribed and de-identified. Participants are not in coercive relationships with the researcher.

Category 2 ‚Äî Small Blood Draws

Blood draw study measuring cortisol levels in first-year medical students

A single 5mL blood draw at one time point from 50 healthy adult volunteers. Draws performed by a licensed phlebotomist. Specimens analyzed for cortisol and processed without identifiers.

Category 7 ‚Äî Research on Individual or Group Behavior

Focus group study on perceived career barriers among first-generation college students

Six focus groups of 6‚Äì8 participants each. Audio recorded. Topics include socioeconomic background, family expectations, and institutional belonging. No sensitive medical/legal disclosures expected.

The 9 Expedited Review Categories (FDA/OHRP)
  • Cat. 1 ‚Äî Clinical studies of drugs/devices already approved, no additional risks
  • Cat. 2 ‚Äî Collection of blood by venipuncture (‚â§550mL/8 weeks; ‚â§2 draws)
  • Cat. 3 ‚Äî Prospective collection of biological specimens (skin, mucosal, hair, nails, excreta)
  • Cat. 4 ‚Äî Collection of data through non-invasive procedures (EEG, MRI, exercise, etc.)
  • Cat. 5 ‚Äî Research involving materials collected solely for non-research purposes
  • Cat. 6 ‚Äî Collection of data through voice/video/digital recordings for research
  • Cat. 7 ‚Äî Research on individual/group characteristics or behavior (surveys, interviews, focus groups) ‚Äî identifiable, not exempt
  • Cat. 8 ‚Äî Continuing review of previously approved research that is now minimal risk
  • Cat. 9 ‚Äî Continuing review when IRB determines remaining work poses no more than minimal risk
‚ö†Ô∏è Key Distinction from Exempt: Expedited research requires a full informed consent document (not just an information sheet) with a participant signature, unless the IRB waives the documentation requirement. The AI reviewer will flag missing consent documentation plans.

FULL BOARD

Full Board Review ‚Äî Greater than Minimal Risk

Research that poses more than minimal risk to participants, involves vulnerable populations, or raises significant ethical questions requiring convened board discussion and vote.

Example Scenarios
Vulnerable Population ‚Äî Incarcerated Individuals

Survey study on mental health needs of incarcerated women at a Connecticut correctional facility

Researcher proposes to administer depression and trauma screening tools to 150 incarcerated women. Even though the survey instrument itself carries minimal risk, the involvement of prisoners automatically triggers Full Board review under 45 CFR 46 Subpart C, requiring at least one prisoner or prisoner representative on the reviewing board.

Greater than Minimal Risk ‚Äî Drug/Intervention Trial

Phase II clinical trial of a novel anxiolytic compound in adult outpatients

A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial administering an investigational drug to 80 adults with generalized anxiety disorder. Involves medical screening, blood draws at 6 time points, adverse event monitoring, and a 6-month follow-up. FDA IND required.

Deception + Psychological Stress Induction

Study inducing acute social rejection to measure cortisol response in adolescents

Participants aged 14‚Äì17 are told they were not chosen for a peer group (Cyberball paradigm). Blood draws before and after. Parental permission and child assent required. Active deception, vulnerable population (minors), and blood draws combine to require Full Board review.

Triggers for Full Board Review
  • Greater than minimal risk ‚Äî risk exceeds what one would encounter in daily life or routine exams
  • Prisoners (Subpart C) ‚Äî any research involving incarcerated individuals
  • Pregnant women / neonates (Subpart B) ‚Äî if research poses risk to fetus or is not for direct benefit
  • Children (Subpart D) ‚Äî research with more than minimal risk and no direct benefit, OR greater than minor increase over minimal risk even with benefit
  • Cognitively impaired adults ‚Äî limited decisional capacity requires additional protections
  • Significant deception ‚Äî when the deception itself poses psychological risk
  • FDA-regulated research ‚Äî IND/IDE studies, drug/device trials
  • IRB chair discretion ‚Äî any protocol raising novel ethical issues may be referred to the full board
üö® Never start Full Board research without written IRB approval. Unlike expedited review (which can proceed if the reviewer doesn't respond within 14 days), Full Board research requires a majority vote at a convened meeting with quorum. Starting early is a serious federal compliance violation.
